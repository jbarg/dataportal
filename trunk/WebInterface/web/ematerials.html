Project Summary

The contemporary field of Materials Science is increasingly focused on the design and optimisation of novel and complex systems with tailored chemical or physical properties. The crucial rTle of computational science in this field is recognised by Foresight, which has identified a need for modelling and simulation to be applied to a wide range of problems facing UK industry. New opportunities from IT and the GRID, together with hardware, algorithmic and scientific developments will, however, promote and integrate technologies for accurate and detailed simulation of systems and problems resulting in effective technology transfer from the strong UK science base to industry. This proposal brings together computer and computational scientists with the aim of exploiting these exciting opportunities by delivering new grid enabled computational technologies for key current areas of materials science relating to:

  • the development of combinatorial materials chemistry, with specific applications to catalysis and ceramics;
  • the prediction of polymorphs of organic-pharmaceutical compounds and their morphology, mechanical and other properties,

both of which are of major importance to contemporary industry, but pose a challenge to basic science and the need to stretch computer science in technologies which harness the coordination and management of complex distributed software systems, and in data management and mining techniques. More specifically, the project will address the following computer / e-Science issues:

  • the management of distributed computations and the computational resources;
  • the application of data mining techniques across multiple distributed databases and other services;
  • the selection and deployment of appropriate middleware-based distribution software architectures;
  • the integration of heterogeneous and distributes data formats for both storage and management;
  • the exploitation of innovative computational paradigms, such as code mobility in the context of grid applications.

Progress in these areas will provide general benefit to the e-Science programme, in addition to the specific benefit to the scientific areas on which we will focus. For more information about the project please go to http://www.e-science.clrc.ac.uk/web/projects/complexmaterials.

As part of the project we are currently putting an initial testbed together, the e-Materials DataPortal. The environment consists of several tools to manage data and launch simulations in on the Grid, namely:

  • The Storage Resource Broker (developed by the San Diego Supercomputing Centre)
  • The Data Portal (developed by CCLRC)
  • The Meta Data Insertion Tool (developed by CCLRC)

The Data Portal is available for project members and 'friends' of the project. If you would like to try out the e-Materials Data Portal please contact the ematerials team for access conditions.

Please note that a number of preparations are necessary before you can first use the e-Materials Data Portal, please download our Introduction guide, which will explain all necessary preparations as well as the usage of the various tools.
